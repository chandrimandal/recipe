Chingri Malai Curry
Ingredient
  • Prawns- 300 grms, shelled & dwveined.
  • Salt to taste.
  • Turmeric Powder- 1/2 teaspoon.
  • Maggi Coconut Milk Powder- 50 grms.
  • Warm Water- 2 1/2 cups.
  • Mustard Oil- 4 teaspoons.
  • Bay Leaf- 1.
  • Green Cardamom- 2.
  • Cinnamom- 1, 1 inch piece.
  • Cloves- 5.
  • Onion- 1 large, chopped.
  • Garlic- 3 cloves, crushed.
  • Ginger Paste- 1 tablespoon.
  • Red Chilli Powder- 1/2 teaspoon.
  • Tomatoes- 1 medium, chopped.
  • Sugar- 1 teaspoon.
  • Green Chillies- 5 slit.
  • Ghee- 1 teaspoon.
  • Garam Masala Powder- 1/2 teaspoon.
Methods
  • Wash the prawns and smear them with a little salt along with 1/4 teaspoon of turmeric powder. Keep them aside.
  • Dissolve the Maggi Coconut Milk Powder in warm water.
  • Heat the mustard oil in a karahi and the bay leaf along with the whole spices. When they start to splutter, add the chopped onion and crushed garlic. Fry the onions just till they turn light brown in colour.
  • Now add the ginger paste, the remaining(1/4) turmeric powder and the red chilli powder. fry for a minute.
  • Add the chopped tomatoes and fry well till cooked. Then, add the marinated prawns and saute for 5 minutes.
  • Add the prepared Coconut Milk, salt, sugar and green chillies. Let it simmer for 8-10 minutes, until the prawns are cooked and the gravy thickens.
  • To finish, add ghee and garam masala powder and remove from fire.
  • Serve Hot with steamed rice.
